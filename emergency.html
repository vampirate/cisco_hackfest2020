Emergency

You have activated the emergency function because you require immediate assistance.
An ambulance will automatically be called to your location in 45 seconds.

Ambulances are needed for life-threatening emergencies, and normal labour may not always be an emergency.
Please consider this before proceeding.

Below are the rare occasions an ambulance may be needed, such as:

  • You are immediately about to give birth with a strong urge to push.
  • Fresh bleeding which is more than an egg cupful (or two changes of pads).
  • Severe abdominal pain that continues and persists after a contraction.
  • The baby's cord is noticeable.
  • Other medical emergencies, such as breathing difficulties or chest pain.
  • There is no one in the immediate vicinity to help and you do not have an alternative mode of transport to the hospital/birthing site
